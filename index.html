Steven Paul Jobs (February 24, 1955 - October 5, 2011)

  • Born in San Francisco and put up for adoption at birth.
  • He was raised in the San Francisco Bay Area in the 1960's by his adoptive parents, Paul and Clara Jobs.
  • After finishing his high school career at Homestead High school, he briefly attended Reed college in 1972 before dropping out.
  • Seeking enlightenment, he travelled through India in 1974 and studied Zen Buddhism.
  • In 1976 Steve Jobs along with Steve Wozniak co-founded Apple Inc.
  • A year later, the Apple 2 was highly successful and followed by the breakthrough Macintosh in 1984.
  • After being forced out of Apple in 1985, he founded NeXT, a computer platform development company specializing in state-of-the-art computers for higher-education and business markets.
  • In addition, Jobs helped to initiate the development of the visual effects industry when he funded Lucasfilm, in 1986. The new company, Pixar, was made possible in part because of Jobs's financial support.
  • In 1997, Apple merged with NeXT, reviving Apple at the verge of bankruptcy and making Jobs CEO of his former company.
  • Beginning the "Think different" advertising campaign, Jobs worked with designer Jonathan Ive to develop a new line of products: the iMac, iTunes and iTunes Store, Apple Store, iPod, iPhone, App Store, and the iPad.
  • Steve Jobs sadly passed away at the age of 56 on October 5, 2011, after being diagnosed with pancreatic cancer in 2003.
